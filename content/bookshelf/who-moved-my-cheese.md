[Who Moved My Cheese?](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Who_Moved_My_Cheese%3F) is a short business fable by Spencer Johnson, first published in 1998. Despite its slim size, it became one of the best-selling business books of all time — a quick read with a lasting point about how we respond to change.

The story follows four characters navigating a maze in search of cheese — a metaphor for whatever you want in life (a job, relationship, security, success). Two mice, Sniff and Scurry, and two tiny people, Hem and Haw, all find a huge stash of cheese and get comfortable. When the cheese disappears one day, their reactions reveal four very different attitudes toward change.

## The four characters

Each character represents a way of dealing with change:

- **Sniff** senses change early and adapts without overthinking it
- **Scurry** takes action immediately, no analysis needed
- **Hem** denies the change, gets angry, and waits for the old cheese to come back
- **Haw** resists at first, but eventually ventures out, learns to laugh at himself, and finds new cheese

Most of us are some mix of Hem and Haw.

## The core message

The lesson is simple: change happens, and clinging to the way things were is the one guaranteed way to lose. The quicker you let go of old cheese, the sooner you find new cheese.

Johnson writes key insights on the walls of the maze as Haw moves through it. A few that stuck with me:

- *"What would you do if you weren't afraid?"*
- *"The quicker you let go of old cheese, the sooner you find new cheese."*
- *"It is safer to search in the maze than remain in a cheeseless situation."*

## Key takeaways

- Change is inevitable — the maze is always moving the cheese
- Anticipating change makes you more adaptable than reacting to it
- Fear of change is usually worse than the change itself
- Small, consistent movement beats paralysis while waiting for things to return to normal
- Letting go of what no longer serves you is how you find what's next
